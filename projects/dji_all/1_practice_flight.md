# Basic Drone Flight Maneuver Drills

A concise visual-line-of-sight flight guide for multirotor training. Each maneuver below has its own short instruction set and its own visual reference for clearer practice.

> [!WARNING]
> Fly only in a legal, open, and controlled area. Maintain visual line of sight, keep clear of people and obstacles, and stop training immediately if control or orientation is lost.

> [!CAUTION]
> Control response depends on aircraft orientation, radio mode, and flight-controller setup. Confirm control directions before takeoff and use a stabilized flight mode for training.

## Circular Orbit

**Purpose:** Build constant-radius orbit control and smooth heading management.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/01_circular-orbit.png)

### Procedure
1. Establish a stable hover over the start point.
2. Select a clear point of interest (POI) on the ground.
3. Enter a slow orbit at a constant radius.
4. Maintain altitude with small, coordinated inputs.
5. Complete one full circle and stop at the start point.

### Standard
- Constant radius
- Stable altitude
- Smooth speed and clean stop

> [!NOTE]
> Practice the same maneuver in both counterclockwise and clockwise directions.

## Figure-Eight

**Purpose:** Build smooth crossover control and equal turning in both directions.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/02_figure-eight.png)

### Procedure
1. Mark two reference points side by side.
2. Start at the midpoint between the two points.
3. Fly the first loop smoothly around the first point.
4. Cross through the center without abrupt corrections.
5. Fly the second loop around the opposite point.
6. Return to the center and stop.

### Standard
- Even loop size
- Smooth center crossover
- Stable altitude throughout

## Ascending and Descending Orbit

**Purpose:** Combine orbit control with deliberate climb and descent management.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/03_ascending-descending-orbit.png)

### Procedure
1. Start at the lower reference altitude.
2. Enter a stable orbit around the POI.
3. Climb gradually while maintaining the orbit.
4. Level briefly at the upper altitude.
5. Descend gradually while keeping the same orbit radius.
6. Return near the starting altitude and stop.

### Standard
- Smooth climb and descent
- Consistent orbit radius
- No abrupt throttle changes

---

## Pendulum or Hourglass

**Purpose:** Practice diagonal tracking and controlled reversals through a center point.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/04_pendulum-hourglass.png)

### Procedure
1. Define four corners forming a box.
2. Start at one corner.
3. Fly diagonally through the center to the opposite corner.
4. Reverse smoothly and pass back through the center.
5. Repeat the diagonal transitions as assigned.
6. Stop at the starting point or center as directed.

### Standard
- Straight diagonal lines
- Stable altitude
- Smooth braking at each reversal

---

## Ascending and Descending Box

**Purpose:** Train straight-line tracking while changing altitude on selected legs.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/05_ascending-descending-box.png)

### Procedure
1. Define a rectangular flight box.
2. Start at the lower reference corner.
3. Fly the first vertical leg while climbing.
4. Fly the top leg level.
5. Fly the opposite vertical leg while descending.
6. Fly the bottom leg level back to the start.

### Standard
- Straight sides
- Level top and bottom legs
- Controlled climb and descent

---

## Nose-In Box

**Purpose:** Train box tracking while maintaining nose-in orientation toward the pilot or reference point.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/06_nose-in-box.png)

### Procedure
1. Establish a stable nose-in hover.
2. Begin the box at low speed.
3. Maintain nose-in orientation on each leg.
4. Use small yaw corrections through the corners.
5. Complete the box and return to the start.

### Standard
- Nose remains pointed toward the pilot/reference
- Clean corners
- Stable altitude and speed

---

## Orbit Around a Point

**Purpose:** Hold a constant orbit around a single reference point.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/07_orbit-around-point.png)

### Procedure
1. Select a clear POI on the ground.
2. Position the aircraft at the planned orbit radius.
3. Begin a slow orbit around the POI.
4. Hold the radius and altitude as constant as possible.
5. Complete one full circle and stop at the start point.

### Standard
- Round path
- Constant distance from the POI
- Smooth orbit speed

---

## External Reference Orbit

**Purpose:** Improve spatial awareness by holding an orbit relative to outside reference markers.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/08_external-reference-orbit.png)

### Procedure
1. Place several visual reference markers around the orbit area.
2. Start at the planned orbit point.
3. Fly the orbit while keeping the path inside the outer markers.
4. Maintain altitude and a consistent radius.
5. Complete the orbit and stop at the start point.

### Standard
- Orbit stays within the boundary markers
- Radius remains consistent
- Aircraft returns accurately to start

---

## Infinity Pattern

**Purpose:** Combine two connected loops into one continuous precision path.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/09_infinity-pattern.png)

### Procedure
1. Identify a center crossover point.
2. Fly the first loop and return to the center.
3. Cross the center smoothly.
4. Fly the second loop in the opposite direction.
5. Continue for the assigned number of cycles.
6. Finish at the center and stop.

### Standard
- Equal loop size
- Smooth center crossover
- Stable altitude and speed

---

## Rectangular Path

**Purpose:** Build basic path discipline on straight legs and square corners.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/10_rectangular-path.png)

### Procedure
1. Define a rectangular flight area.
2. Start at one corner.
3. Fly each side in sequence at constant altitude.
4. Use smooth, deliberate turns at each corner.
5. Return to the start point and stop.

### Standard
- Straight legs
- Near-90° corners
- Consistent altitude

---

## Vertical Step

**Purpose:** Train precise climbs, descents, and hover holds at fixed levels.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/11_vertical-step.png)

### Procedure
1. Start in a stable hover.
2. Climb to the first height and hold.
3. Descend to the next level and hold.
4. Continue the step pattern as assigned.
5. Finish at the designated level and stabilize.

### Standard
- Clean vertical alignment
- Clear brake/hold at each level
- No drifting during the hold

---

## Vertical Zigzag

**Purpose:** Combine forward flight with alternating climb and descent angles.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/12_vertical-zigzag.png)

### Procedure
1. Start at the marked point.
2. Climb diagonally to the first peak.
3. Descend diagonally to the next low point.
4. Repeat the zigzag pattern with steady spacing.
5. Finish near the assigned endpoint.

### Standard
- Consistent angles
- Smooth direction changes
- Stable rhythm through the pattern

---

## Vertical Wave

**Purpose:** Develop smooth, continuous up-and-down path control.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/13_vertical-wave.png)

### Procedure
1. Begin at the start point.
2. Fly forward while climbing smoothly.
3. Round the crest without stopping.
4. Continue into a smooth descent and trough.
5. Repeat the wave pattern with even spacing.

### Standard
- Rounded transitions
- Consistent amplitude
- Smooth forward pace

---

## Ground Handling

**Purpose:** Practice minimal-power straight-line surface movement when the aircraft and site permit it.  
**Visual:** [Open maneuver diagram](assets/14_ground-handling.png)

### Procedure
1. Confirm the surface is clear and suitable.
2. Arm only when instructed.
3. Apply minimum power to begin movement.
4. Track in a straight line to the stop point.
5. Stop cleanly and disarm immediately.

### Standard
- Minimal throttle use
- Straight tracking
- Immediate disarm after completion

> [!WARNING]
> Skip this exercise if the aircraft is not suitable for ground movement. Taxiing a multirotor can lead to tip-over or propeller strike.

## Safety Checklist

### Before Flight
- Airframe and propellers inspected
- Battery secured and adequately charged
- Control directions confirmed
- Flight mode verified
- Boundaries and emergency landing area defined

### During Flight
- Maintain visual line of sight
- Use small control inputs
- Keep safe distance from people and obstacles
- Land immediately if control response becomes abnormal

### After Flight
- Disarm before approach
- Disconnect the battery
- Check motors, ESCs, and propellers
- Record training notes and observed errors
