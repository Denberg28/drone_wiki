# Powered by Hivemind: Autonomous Teaming and Strike at the Edge

> GitHub-ready summary based on Shield AI’s case study: **“Powered by Hivemind: Autonomous Teaming and Strike at the Edge.”**  
> Source: Shield AI — https://shield.ai/powered-by-hivemind-autonomous-teaming-and-strike-at-the-edge/

---

## Overview

Shield AI’s case study describes how **Hivemind**, its autonomy software, was integrated with the **Destinus Hornet** platform as a testbed for future strike and interceptor systems. The project explored how autonomous systems can operate in contested environments where GPS, communications, and manual replanning may be degraded.

The partnership between **Shield AI** and **Destinus** is intended to support future integration of Hivemind onto the **Destinus Ruta**, a low-cost turbojet deep-strike cruise missile designed for contested and GPS/GNSS-denied environments.

---

## Key Capabilities Demonstrated

- Rapid integration of mission autonomy onto an existing air platform
- Autonomous flight control and mission execution
- Geofenced keep-in and keep-out zone compliance
- Autonomous altitude and airspeed management
- Teaming between Hornet and V-BAT platforms
- In-flight target and mission updates
- Low-altitude terrain-following route replanning
- ISR-to-strike mission coordination
- Human oversight retained throughout the campaign

---

## Phase 1: Hivemind on Hornet

The first phase focused on integrating Hivemind with the **Hornet flight control and mission systems**. Shield AI and Destinus reportedly completed this integration in under two months by using Hivemind’s modular architecture and Destinus’ common platform design.

Hornet was used as a surrogate testbed for future interceptor and strike systems. Initial sorties validated basic autonomous flight circuits, followed by more complex mission behaviors such as:

- Keep-in geofenced zones
- Keep-out geofenced zones
- Autonomous altitude control
- Autonomous airspeed control

---

## Phase 2: Teaming Hornets and V-BATs

The second phase expanded the demonstration from single-aircraft autonomy to **small-team autonomous operations**.

In this mission configuration:

- **V-BAT** acted as an ISR asset
- **V-BAT** also served as an airborne communications relay
- Hornet platforms received in-flight target updates
- Hivemind coordinated timing and formation
- Multiple Hornets executed a synchronized engagement profile from multiple axes

A key result was that Hivemind-enabled Hornet aircraft could receive target updates from V-BAT and replan routes in real time, including low-altitude terrain-following paths. The case study emphasizes that the decision to engage remained with the human operator.

---

## Phase 3: Reconnaissance-to-Strike Loop

The third phase tested a fuller mission thread in Spain, closer to the intended Ruta mission set. This phase validated a new autonomy-assisted mission planning system and expanded terrain-following capabilities.

The demonstrated sequence included:

1. Autonomy-assisted mission planning at the ground control station
2. Radio and datalink testing
3. Autonomous terrain following
4. In-flight mission and target updates
5. Commanded terminal dive onto an operator-designated target

This phase showed how Hivemind could help connect reconnaissance, communication relay, mission replanning, and strike execution across heterogeneous unmanned systems.

---

## Why It Matters

The case study presents Hivemind as a way to upgrade existing strike, interceptor, and ISR assets into coordinated autonomous teams without requiring full airframe redesign.

The main operational claims are:

- Existing platforms can be adapted quickly
- Heterogeneous systems can coordinate as a team
- Mission autonomy can support contested environments
- Operators can retain human-on-the-loop command authority
- Mission planning and retasking can be streamlined
- Autonomous teaming can reduce operator workload

Shield AI and Destinus plan to continue the work by integrating Hivemind onto the **Destinus Ruta** platform.
